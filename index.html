Are you struggling with math?

You are not alone. Studies show that over 50% of people taking college level algebra receive a grade lower than C (Ganter & Haver, 2011), which can prevent completion of undergraduate programs in many STEM and business fields. Most students who struggle with these courses fail because their understanding of foundational mathematics skills is insufficient.

Take control

Studies also show that students who use sources outside the classroom, such as tutoring and math labs, earn higher grades and maintain better academic standing than those who do not (Cooper, 2007). Tutoring gives you the opportunity to improve your understanding of mathematics, therefore setting yourself apart from fellow classmates.

Is tutoring right for you

From returning students who haven't opened a textbook in years, to ex-military students, or recent high school graduates who need a solid foundation, all types of students will benefit from tutoring as it is the best way to develop a deeper understanding of foundational mathematics skills.
