Remedial Math

Tutoring in Remedial Math is well suited for students who want a strong foundation in beginner and intermediate algebra. Subjects covered can include but are not limited to: expressions, systems of equations, functions, real numbers, inequalities, exponents, quadratics, polynomials, radical and rational expressions. Tutoring in remedial math is a great way to prepare for MATH1314 College Algebra or as a refresher before taking the T.S.I. or ACT Tests.

College Algebra

Tutoring in College Algebra is well suited for students currently enrolled in MATH1314 College Algebra or those who want a refresher before taking Pre-Calculus. It can coincide with subjects taught in MATH1314 or can be split apart to target specific subjects including: graph transformations, quadratic functions and their graphs, polynomial functions and their graphs, rational functions and their graphs, vertex form, logarithmic functions, exponential functions, asymptotes, and series and sequences. Taken alongside MATH1314 tutoring in College Algebra can be your best tool to understanding College Algebra concepts and material.

Pre-Calculus

Tutoring in Pre-Calculus is well suited for studenta currently enrolled in MATH2412 Pre-Calculus or those who want a refresher before taking Calculus. It can coincide with subjects taught in MATH2412 or be split apart to target specific subjects including: systems of equations, fraction decomposistion, right triangle trigonometry, graphs of trig functions, solving and verifying trig equations, law of sines and cosines, 2D and 3D vectors, working with conics, parametric equations, and the polar coordinate system. Taken alongside MATH2412 tutoring in Pre-Calculus can be an excellent way to build a strong foundation for future calculus classes.

Calculus I

Tutoring in Calculus I is well suited for students currently enrolled in MATH2413 Calculus I or those who want a refresher before taking Calculus II. It can coincide with subjects taught in MATH2413 or be split apart to target specific subjects including: limits, continuity, derivatives and their rules, related rates, indeterminate forms, finding maximums and minimums, curve sketching, optimization problems, antiderivatives, and basic integration. Taken alongside MATH2413 tutoring in Calculus I can help students establish a strong foundation for future calculus and engineering classes.

HTML5 and CSS3

Tutoring in HTML5 and CSS3 is well suited for students currently enrolled in ITSE1301 Intro to Web Development. Topics covered can include: introduction to HTML and CSS, page structure and layout, web styles with CSS, tables and columns, web forms, special effects, designing print pages, and designing sites for mobile platforms. Taken alongside ITSE1301 this can be an excellent resource to understanding basic web development concepts.

Intro to Programming with Javascript

Tutoring in Javascript is well suited for students currently enrolled in ITSE2302 Intro to Javascript. Topics covered can include: introduction to programming, Javascript basics, problem solving with programming, variables and operators, selection structures, repetition structures, advanced decisions and loops, web forms, source file use, arrays, and searching and sorting. Taken alongside ITSE2302 this can be an excellent resource to understanding basic programming concepts.
